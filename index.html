Ideal Client Profile

Internal qualification tool · who to pursue, who to avoid, and how to tell in 60 seconds.

01 · The Snapshot

Who We’re Going After

Our ideal client is an Irish SME doing €1M–€10M in revenue that has outgrown its finance function. The founder is still managing the numbers themselves – or relying on one junior person – and knows it’s a problem but hasn’t solved it because hiring a full-time Finance Director feels too expensive. They already have an external accountant for year-end filing and make decisions fast without a board.

Sernon lens: Every GREEN CRI client is a potential Sernon client. If the founder is accumulating personal wealth, equity, or property – flag it. A CRI engagement that converts to a Sernon relationship is the ideal outcome across both businesses.
02 · Qualification Criteria

Traffic Light Assessment

Green – Pursue
  • Annual revenue €1M–€10M
  • Growing fast, finance behind the curve
  • No finance hire or only a junior bookkeeper
  • Founder-led, single fast decision maker
  • Existing external accountant in place
  • Net profit ≥ €100k (comfortably sustains €3k+/month)
  • Services, tech, construction, or trades
  • Ireland-based or remote-friendly
Amber – Qualify Further
  • Revenue under €1M but high growth trajectory
  • Part-time finance person already in place
  • Manufacturing sector
  • Heavy physical presence required
  • Decision making involves multiple stakeholders or a board
Action: Book a discovery call to clarify. If 2+ AMBER criteria remain unresolved after that call, downgrade to RED and move on.
Red – Do Not Pursue
  • Under €1M revenue with no growth path
  • Net profit under €40k annually
  • Wants bookkeeping / invoicing only
  • Wants annual accounts prepared only
  • No existing accountant (too early stage)
  • Requires full-time on-site presence
03 · Qualifying Questions

First Conversation – Eight Questions

1
“How is your finance function set up at the moment?”
Listening for: no dedicated hire, founder doing it themselves, or one junior person – this is the green flag.
2
“What does your turnover look like this year compared to last year?”
Listening for: growth trajectory – fast-growing businesses are the target. Flat or declining revenue is a red flag.
3
“When did you last have a clear picture of your monthly numbers?”
Listening for: pain – if they hesitate or say “not recently” or “the accountant does it at year end”, that’s the problem CRI solves.
4
“How’s the business performing – are you comfortable with where the margins are?”
Listening for: ability to pay – surfaces profitability early before you invest time reviewing accounts. If they hesitate or signal thin margins, probe further before progressing.
5
“Have you looked at bringing someone in-house for finance?”
Listening for: awareness of the problem and the cost barrier – “yeah but it’s expensive” is exactly the door CRI walks through.
6
“Who handles your year-end accounts at the moment?”
Listening for: existing accountant relationship – confirms they are established and identifies a potential referral source.
7
“How many people are in the business at the moment?”
Listening for: small team, lean headcount – confirms they haven’t built out a finance function internally.
8
“Are you the one making the call on something like this?”
Listening for: single decision maker – if there’s a board or multiple partners the sales cycle gets complicated fast.
5+ green signals = pursue
|
Mixed = book a second call
|
3+ red flags = walk away

05 · Engagement Filter

The 3-Month Commitment Rule

Minimum 3-month Letter of Engagement – non-negotiable. If a prospect wants to “try one month” or won’t commit to 3 months, they are not a fit. This protects CRI’s investment in the initial deep-dive phase. Minimum engagement value: €9,000.

If they push back
“The first three months is when we do the heavy lifting. It takes that long to properly understand your numbers. After that, you’ll wonder how you managed without us.”
Onboarding reality: Expect 1–3 months from signed LOE to fully operational, depending on the state of the client’s books. Messy books = longer ramp. Set this expectation with the client upfront – it avoids frustration and protects the relationship during the deep-dive phase.
06 · Network Activation

Where Clients Actually Come From

Every CRI client to date has come from someone on the team’s personal network. This is the primary pipeline source – treat it deliberately, not accidentally.

Do This Quarterly
The Exercise

Each team member (Simon, Luke, Eamon) sits down and reviews their personal and professional network – LinkedIn, phone contacts, GAA, events, old colleagues. One question: “Who do I already know that’s running a business doing €1M+ who’s probably struggling with their finance function?”

Target: each person identifies 10–15 contacts per quarter. Log them straight into the pipeline tracker. First round: within 2 weeks of receiving this document.

Conversation Script
How to Approach

These are people who already know and trust you. This is not cold outreach – it’s a conversation with someone you have a relationship with. Open with genuine interest in their business, then steer naturally toward how they’re managing the finance side.

“I was thinking about you – how’s the business going? Are you still doing the books yourself or have you sorted that out?”

Ownership Map
Who Owns What

Simon: Irish business network, football contacts, direct relationships.
Luke: UK connections, LinkedIn network, tech/software contacts.
Eamon & Hesh: Operational and delivery-side relationships.

Play to each person’s strengths. Luke opens doors, Simon closes. Eamon & Hesh manage relationships where trust and patience matter.

07 · Referral Channels

The Medium-Term Build

These channels need relationship-building before they produce leads. Start developing them now – they become the pipeline engine once the personal network is systematically worked.

Accountancy Firms

Approach the partner directly. Position CRI as additive – not a threat. CRI handles the ongoing finance function; the accountant keeps year-end filing and stays the trusted advisor.

“Your clients who’ve grown quickly probably need more than a year-end visit. We handle everything in between – you stay as their accountant.”

Solicitors

They see business transactions, disputes, and growth events. Any client going through a fundraise, acquisition, or rapid expansion likely needs a finance function. Warm introduction request only.

Ask for introductions to clients navigating growth, transactions, or expansion. Do not cold-approach.

Existing Clients

Ask directly: “Do you know any other founders in a similar position to where you were when we started?” Time this after the first meaningful win is delivered – not before.

Only ask once CRI has demonstrated tangible value. The client needs to have something specific to point to.
